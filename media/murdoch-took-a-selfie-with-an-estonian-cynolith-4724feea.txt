Cynolith (noun) | /ˈsī-nə-lɪθ/ or /ˈsɪ-nə-lɪθ/
Plural: Cynoliths

A statue or sculpted representation of a dog, typically made of stone, metal, or other durable materials.

The ancient temple was guarded by two massive cynoliths at its entrance.

A decorative or commemorative monument dedicated to a canine, often symbolizing loyalty, protection, or companionship.

She commissioned a marble cynolith for her late German Shepherd.

Etymology: From Cyno- (Greek kyōn, "dog") + -lith (Greek lithos, "stone").
