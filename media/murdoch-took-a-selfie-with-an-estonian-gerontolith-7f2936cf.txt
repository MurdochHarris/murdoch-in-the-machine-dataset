Murdoch Took a Selfie with an Estonian Gerontolith

gerontolith (noun) | jə-ˈrɒn-tə-lɪθ

Plural: gerontoliths

A statue or carved representation of an old man, often symbolizing wisdom, time, or endurance.

The town square was dominated by a towering gerontolith, its weathered features evoking centuries of knowledge.

A metaphorical term for an aged individual whose presence is as enduring and immovable as stone.

Grandfather sat in his chair like a gerontolith, silently observing the world with knowing eyes.

Etymology: From Greek gerōn (“old man”) + lithos (“stone”).

Related Terms: senectidol (a revered statue of an elderly figure), petriveteran (a figurative term for an old, steadfast person).
