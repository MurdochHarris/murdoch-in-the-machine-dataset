Murdoch Working as a Hebesoter (i.e. Lifeguard) in Colorado

Hebesoter (noun) [hee-bə-SOH-ter]

Plural: Hebesoters

Etymology: From Greek hebe- (youth) + soter (savior, rescuer).

Definition:

A young individual, typically in their teens or early twenties, who serves as a lifeguard, responsible for ensuring the safety of swimmers and rescuing those in distress.

(By extension) Any youthful protector or guardian, especially in aquatic environments.

Example Usage:

"During the summer, the beach was patrolled by skilled heboseters trained in water rescue."

"The pool's heboseter quickly spotted the struggling child and acted immediately."
